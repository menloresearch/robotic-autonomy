# Safety — 08 Apr 2026

## What I did
- Reviewed current safety benchmark metrics and compared them against AV-style safety benchmark practices
- Mapped existing benchmark coverage:
  - HIC15
  - Peak head acceleration
  - Nij
  - ISO/TS 15066 child-scaled body-region force limits
  - Damage level scoring (SAFE → CRITICAL)
  - Safety boundary plane
- Defined critical gaps between current work and AV-inspired benchmark design:
  - Missing pre-collision risk metrics
  - Missing robot behavior / policy quality metrics
  - Missing reaction-time and stopping evaluation
  - Missing probabilistic risk estimation across repeated trials
  - Missing structured scenario coverage tracking
  - Missing adversarial / stress-test scenario generation
- Proposed AV-inspired additions for next iteration:
  - TTC (Time-To-Collision)
  - Minimum distance
  - Safety margin = TTC - stopping time
  - Velocity / jerk near human
  - Detection time / stop time
  - Injury probability across repeated runs
  - Scenario taxonomy + coverage metric
  - Adversarial scenario search

## Findings / results
- Current benchmark is strong on **post-collision injury evaluation** but weak on **pre-collision safety evaluation**
- Current system can measure how severe an impact is, but cannot yet measure how avoidable the impact was
- Main benchmark gap is that unsafe robot behavior can still be rated safe if contact barely does not happen
- AV benchmark ideas show that safety should be evaluated in three stages:
  - before collision
  - during collision
  - after collision
- The highest-priority upgrade is adding:
  - TTC
  - minimum distance
  - stopping / reaction capability
- These additions would shift the benchmark from **damage measurement** to **true safety evaluation**
